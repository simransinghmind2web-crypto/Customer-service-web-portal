🔄 ISS PROCESS FLOW

Standard In-Service Support workflow — CFSATE to Aerotrain Corp.

1

Snag Occurs on Trainer

A technical fault, malfunction, or deficiency is identified on a CFSATE trainer unit during operations or routine inspection.

CFSATE
2

Snag Reported on ISS Form

The CFSATE representative logs the snag on the In-Service Support Form, detailing the nature of the fault, affected system, and urgency level.

CFSATE
3

ISS Form Verified & Emailed to Aerotrain Corp

The completed ISS form is verified and dispatched to Aerotrain Corp via the official support channel: ISS@aerotraincorp.com

CFSATE
4

Aerotrain Acknowledges & Assigns Ticket

Aerotrain Corp acknowledges receipt, assigns a unique ticket number, and confirms the scheduled date and time of visit to CFSATE for remediation.

AEROTRAIN CORP
5

Aerotrain Rep Visits CFSATE & Fixes Snag

An Aerotrain engineer visits the facility and carries out the necessary repair or corrective action on the trainer system.

AEROTRAIN CORP
6

Aerotrain Documents Fix in ISS Form

The Aerotrain representative writes a detailed brief of the corrective action taken, parts replaced, and resolution notes directly in the ISS form.

AEROTRAIN CORP
7

CFSATE Verifies & Signs Off Fix

The CFSATE representative verifies that the fix is satisfactory and functional, then signs off on the ISS form as confirmation of acceptance.

CFSATE
8

Form Entered in Trainer Log

The completed ISS form is entered into the Trainer Log at CFSATE and a copy is archived at Aerotrain Corp for traceability and compliance records.

BOTH PARTIES
9

✅ Ticket Closed

The support ticket is formally closed. All records are preserved. Snag resolution is complete.
